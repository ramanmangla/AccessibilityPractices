Web Accessibility

An essential yet often forgotten website feature.

Why Accessibility?

As a web dev, it’s important to ensure you don’t lock anyone out of your website. You can do this is a number of ways - adhering to best practices, do cross browser testing, and considering accessibility from the start.

Accessible sites can be accessed by anyone, including users with physical or mental impairments, regardless of how they access the site as well (i.e. keyboard, mouse, touch screen, voice assistants like Alexa or Google Home).

Types of Disabilities

  • Visual impairments:

    • blindness, low level vision, colour blindness
    • support the use of screen magnifiers and screen readers
    • 285 million people are estimated to be visually impaired worldwide - WHO

  • Hearing impairments:

    • either low level hearing or no hearing at all
    • text alternatives to audio content that can be read or captions for videos
    • 466 million people worldwide - WHO

  • Mobility impairments:

    • difficulty making exact movements with mouse/head pointer
    • needs to be keyboard accessible
    • 16.1% of adults affected - WHO

  • Cognitive impairments:

    • delivering content in more than one way (text-to-speech, video)
    • minimizing distractions (unnecessary content/advertisments)
    • dividing processes into logical steps

Visual Impairment

Web experiences based inherently on visual commonly creates unreadable combinations of fonts and colours.

Users with good vision can find these web pages difficult to read and or irritating to the eyes.

Users with visual impairment that suffer from colour blindness, low vision, or blindness cannot navigate these web pages at all.

Solution

  • Provide contrast between colors and textures
  • Allow manual font size adjustment
  • Prioritize the colors and fonts in the interface
  • Provide a description for non-text content
  • Use heading in organizing the content
  • Grant keyboard shortcuts

Hearing impairements

Many web pages today are accessible to people with hearing impairements because the information is presented in a visual way (with images and text).

Auditory diabilies include deafness, hearing loss, anacusis, hard of hearing or hearing impairment

Solution

Implementing options for the hearing impairment are really easy and inexpensive, common solutions are:

  • Adding captions whenever a sound appears
  • Sign language for live content

Mobility impairements

Mobility impairment is a broad category of physical disabilities that may range in severity from mild loss of fine-motor control to quadraplegia.

Users may have limited arm or hand movement, use just one hand, have a tremor, have difficulty with fine movements, or be unable to hold a mouse

Solution

Given the range of mobility/dexterity disabilities, a wide variety of assistive technologies are available for these users including:

  • Ensuring that all functions can be accessed with the keyboard.
  • Using a logical tab order (left to right, top to bottom or as appropriate for locale).
  • Providing methods such as a skip link to navigate over menus and navigation bars.
  • Being careful with the use of shortcut keys as many of them conflict with assistive devices or browser functions.
  • Providing logical and standard keyboard commands for interacting with the product.
